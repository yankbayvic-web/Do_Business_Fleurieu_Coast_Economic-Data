Strategic Insights
Agriculture Dominance

Agriculture accounts for 28.6% of total output ($174.6M) and is the largest employer at 348 jobs, yet pays the lowest average wages (~$28.3K/job). High economic output, low wage return — a structural vulnerability for household income.

Tourism Engine

Accommodation & Food is the 2nd largest employer at 222 jobs (13.5%) and generates $17.8M in regional exports — the strongest tourism export signal in the region. It is the sector with the greatest capacity for job-rich growth.

Export Concentration Risk

Agriculture generates 57.2% of all regional exports ($142.8M of $249.5M). A single sector driving over half the export base is a significant structural risk to regional economic resilience.

Real Estate Value Trap

Real Estate has the highest value-add ratio (76%) and $69.9M output — but only 29 jobs. This reflects land and asset values, not productive employment. Not a sector for job-creation investment.

Health & Social Opportunity

Health & Social Assistance pays ~$103.5K average per job — the highest wage accessible to local residents. 165 jobs and growing. Expanding health services would lift median household income significantly.

Stable but Low Job Growth

Employment has remained flat at 1,639 jobs across both REMPLAN releases. Economic output has grown ~6% but no new jobs have been created. Productivity is rising without workforce expansion.

Construction Strength

Construction is the 3rd largest sector at $77.8M output, 143 jobs and $31.4M local sales. Activity likely driven by residential demand — an indicator of population growth confidence.

Trade Surplus Growing

The regional trade surplus improved from $66.5M to $70.5M — exports growing faster than imports signals a net outward flow of economic value and improving self-sufficiency.
